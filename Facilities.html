OUR FACILITIES








Classrooms



The classroom is the best place to learn different things. These Classrooms just how fun learning is and inspire
kids everywhere. "The classroom should be an entrance into the world, not an escape from it. -John Ciardi.


















Sports



Muslim Community University (MCU) has a comprehensive range of sporting facilities on-site. The grassed oval and Aquatic Precinct
together with tennis courts, Basketball court, Football pitch, Volleyball court and EA Sports games are available for students to use
whilst participating in some of the 47 co-curricula activities on offer. The nearby Brisbane River also offers a superb training site for
Swimming. As part of the School’s Master Plan, a new multi-purpose learning and sports facility are in the planning stages.
















Performing Arts and Theatre



Art classrooms are complemented by a sculpture courtyard and kiln and support the School’s long tradition of creating, acquiring and displaying
works of fine art. Performing arts events are very well catered for with the 485-seat, air-conditioned theatre, tree-top level Green Room and
spacious foyer. The main theatre can also be divided, by means of operable walls, into two smaller theatres; each seating 150, and a perfor
-mance area comprising the stage and floor area.














Mosques

The Muslim Community University Mosque was built in 1962, is available for students,staff and vistors at any time for prayer. The mosque was
designed by the best archteict in world (GIWA Architecture Limited). The mosque contain Hundreds of Thousands of people at once.














Library

The Library features three spacious teaching areas and two adjoining multimedia classrooms, each with wireless data projectors. A key
feature of the Library is the ‘chill-out’ couch with views into the Mt Coot-tha native Australian bush. This area is much loved by the girls
in need of a quiet space. Housing an expansive collection of fiction, non-fiction, audio and video resources, the Library has full wireless
access, making a multitude of online resources accessible 24/7. In the evening, the Library provides the ideal location for study and
research for Boarders as well as day students participating in the Extended School Day Program.














Hostels

The Muslim Community University First Hostels was built in 1963, and was restructed by one of the best Architecture in world, in 2010.
