EMPLOYMENT














Muslim Community University (MCU) offers full time and part time employment
opportunities for teachers and non-teaching staff.



Muslim Community University (MCU) is a Muslim, independent University for Students Across the globe. The University has a wonderful reputation as a vibrant and
compassionate community which encompasses the values of learning, community, social justice and personal growth that empowers our students. MCU provides a
supportive and engaging work environment with caring and professional staff where dedication, creativity and hard work are acknowledged and valued.


Employment benefits Include:


  • Access to comprehensive salary packaging options
  • Opportunities for professional development
  • Generous superannuation scheme
  • Discounted Stuartholme School fees
  • Long service leave accessibility after 7 years
  • Access to the pool
  • Cinema club evenings
  • Book and DVD borrowing services at our library
  • Onsite car parking
  • Discounted onsite core fitness classes
  • Social breakfasts and end of term functions
  • Social sport opportunities
  • Access to an onsite Registered Nurse and discounted annual flu vaccinations

    • Our Commitment TO Student Protection


    Muslim Community University (MCU) is committed to recruiting, selecting, training and managing employees in such a way that limits risks to students. The University
    adheres to policies and procedures in relation to employment which are contained in the Working with Children (Risk Management and Screening) Act 2000, Working
    with Children (Risk Management and Screening) Regulation 2020, Education (Accreditation of Non-State University) Act 2017 and Regulation and the Education
    (University College of Teachers) Act 2005. All candidates are required to undergo a screening process prior to appointment and the successful candidate must comply
    with University policies and relevant legislation relating to student protection.It is a requirement under the Working with Children (Risk Management and Screening)
    Act 2000 that people working with children (under 18 years of age) in a paid, voluntary, or self-employed capacity, are required to undergo a criminal history check
    the “working with children check”. A certified copy, or sighting of the original Blue Card, must be produced prior to commencement of duties.
